Dr. Tushar Anil Warke

Dr. Tushar Warke is a highly experienced Nephrologist in Wakad, Pune, known for his compassionate care and advanced clinical expertise in kidney diseases. He completed MBBS from GS Govt. Medical College & KEM Hospital, Mumbai, MD (Medicine) from Grant Govt. Medical College & JJ Hospital, Mumbai, and superspecialisation DNB (Nephrology) from Sir Ganga Ram Hospital, New Delhi (2015) — awarded the prestigious Gold Medal (All-India First). With 17+ years of experience, he specializes in chronic kidney disease management, dialysis care, kidney transplant evaluation and follow-up, and renal complications of diabetes and hypertension. A keen academic and teacher, he has trained nephrologists and presented work at national and international conferences.

  • Comprehensive CKD care — staged management, slowing progression & complication control.
  • Dialysis services — hemodialysis planning, access care & anemia–bone mineral management.
  • Kidney transplant — pre-transplant evaluation, immunosuppression protocols & long-term follow-up.
  • Diabetic & hypertensive kidney disease — risk reduction, medication optimisation & lifestyle plans.

Professional Skills

Evidence-based nephrology with focus on early diagnosis, fluid–electrolyte balance, resistant hypertension, acute kidney injury (AKI), glomerular diseases, and holistic CKD nutrition & vaccination protocols.

Education & Achievements

Strong academic foundation with national recognition; active contributor to nephrology training and research presentations.

  • DNB (Nephrology), 2015 – Sir Ganga Ram Hospital, New Delhi — Gold Medal, All-India First.
  • MD (Medicine) – Grant Govt. Medical College & JJ Hospital, Mumbai.
  • MBBS – GS Govt. Medical College & KEM Hospital, Mumbai.
  • Academics – Trained many nephrologists; presentations at national & international conferences.

Conditions & Care

Consult for precise diagnosis, clear counselling, and long-term renal protection plans tailored to your needs.

  • AKI, CKD Stages 1–5, ESRD care pathways.
  • Glomerulonephritis, nephrotic syndrome, lupus nephritis.
  • Electrolyte & acid–base disorders, resistant hypertension.
  • Post-transplant monitoring, infection prophylaxis & rejection surveillance.
