Bariatric Surgery in Tathawade, PCMC — Safe, Structured Weight-Loss & Metabolic Care

We offer comprehensive bariatric & metabolic surgery programs—covering evaluation, laparoscopic procedures, nutrition, and long-term follow-up. Our team helps patients with obesity and obesity-related conditions achieve sustainable weight loss and better health with the support of 24×7 Emergency, ICU, dieticians, and physiotherapy.

Who Is Bariatric Surgery For?

  • BMI ≥ 40, or BMI ≥ 35 with comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, sleep apnoea, fatty liver, joint pain).
  • Failed meaningful weight loss with supervised diet, exercise, and medication.
  • Adults fit for anaesthesia after cardiac, endocrine, and nutritional assessment.

Procedures We Perform (Laparoscopic)

  • Sleeve Gastrectomy: Reduces stomach size to control hunger and intake.
  • Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass: Restrictive + malabsorptive; effective for diabetes remission.
  • Mini/One-Anastomosis Gastric Bypass (OAGB): Selected cases for higher BMI/diabetes.
  • Revision Bariatric Surgery: For weight regain or reflux after prior surgery (case-based).

Pre-operative Workup

  • Detailed medical history, BMI & body composition, dietary review, psychological readiness.
  • Blood tests (CBC, LFT, RFT, HbA1c, lipids, vitamin D/B12, thyroid), ECG, Chest X-ray/2D Echo as indicated.
  • GI evaluation (upper GI endoscopy/USG) where required.
  • Dietician counselling on pre-op liver-shrink diet and post-op staged diet.

Recovery & Follow-Up Pathway

  • Hospital stay usually 2–3 days; early mobilisation the same day/next morning.
  • Liquid → soft → regular protein-rich diet under dietician supervision.
  • Vitamin/mineral supplementation, wound care, and activity progression plan.
  • Scheduled reviews at 2 weeks, 6 weeks, 3/6/12 months, then yearly.

Expected Benefits

  • Significant weight loss over 12–18 months, plateau thereafter.
  • Improvement/remission in type-2 diabetes, hypertension, dyslipidaemia, sleep apnoea, PCOS, fatty liver.
  • Better mobility, joint relief, energy, and quality of life.

Risks & Safety

  • Early: bleeding, leak, infection, DVT/PE (rare; minimised by protocols).
  • Late: nutritional deficiencies, reflux, gallstones, weight regain without lifestyle adherence.
  • Our pathway includes VTE prophylaxis, leak checks, pain control, and education to reduce risks.

Why Choose Pulse Hospital

  • Experienced laparoscopic & bariatric surgeons with anaesthesia, ICU, and physician backup.
  • Standardised ERAS protocols for faster recovery and shorter stay.
  • Integrated program: dietician, physiotherapy, psychology, and long-term follow-up.
  • Cashless/TPA assistance and transparent counselling for procedure selection.
